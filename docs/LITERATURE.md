# Literature

Index of reference material. One entry per work; per-paper notes live in `docs/papers/` once read. Status: **queued / reading / noted / skipped**.

Reading order: Tetlock → Lou et al. → Welch & Goyal → agent-based modeling material.

## Trade window (premarket → close)

| Work | Status | Why it matters | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lou, Polk & Skouras (2019), *A Tug of War: Overnight vs. Intraday Expected Returns*, JFE | queued | Overnight and intraday returns behave as two markets with distinct clienteles — direct empirical support for the cluster hypothesis, mapped onto our exact trade window | [Author PDF](https://personal.lse.ac.uk/polk/research/TugOfWar.pdf) · [LSE repository](https://eprints.lse.ac.uk/87481/7/Polk_Tug%20of%20War.pdf) |
| Gao, Han, Li & Zhou (2018), *Market Intraday Momentum*, JFE | queued | First-half-hour SPY returns predict last-half-hour returns; candidate signal inside our horizon | SSRN / Google Scholar |

## Sentiment as a signal

| Work | Status | Why it matters | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tetlock (2007), *Giving Content to Investor Sentiment*, JF | queued | Media pessimism predicts daily index pressure then reversion — closest published analogue to our design; shortest, most intuitive read | [SSRN working paper](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=685145) |
| Baker & Wurgler (2006), *Investor Sentiment and the Cross-Section of Stock Returns*, JF | queued | Standard sentiment index construction; warning: sentiment predicts mainly hard-to-arbitrage stocks, not SPY/QQQ | SSRN / Google Scholar |
| Da, Engelberg & Gao (2015), *The Sum of All FEARS*, RFS | queued | Search-volume sentiment predicting short-horizon reversals; possible free proxy source | SSRN / Google Scholar |

## Heterogeneous agents & clusters

| Work | Status | Why it matters | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brock & Hommes (1998), *Heterogeneous Beliefs and Routes to Chaos*, JEDC | queued | Founding formal model of fundamentalist/chartist regime switching — the theoretical core of the cluster idea; dense | Google Scholar |
| Hommes (2006), *Heterogeneous Agent Models in Economics and Finance*, Handbook of Computational Economics | queued | Free survey substitute for the Hommes book; covers the same lineage | Google Scholar (free PDF circulates) |

## Access notes

- Everything above has a free working-paper version; search title on **SSRN** or **Google Scholar** and prefer author-hosted or SSRN PDFs
- NBER versions are paywalled but almost always have a free SSRN twin
- No book purchases required for this phase
